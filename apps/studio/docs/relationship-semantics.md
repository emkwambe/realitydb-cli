# Relationship Semantics

In RealityDB Studio, relationships are color-coded based on their **behavioral meaning**. This helps you "read" the diagram as a story of data flow.

## Semantic Types

### 🔵 Connection (Blue)
*Standard Relational Link*
The most basic relationship. Indicates that one table references another (e.g., `Customers` → `Orders`). It implies ownership or association without specific side effects.

### 🟣 Trigger (Purple)
*Cascade Behavior*
Indicates that an action in the source table causes an event in the target table.
*Example: `Orders` → `Shipments`. Creating an order triggers the creation of a shipment.*

### 🟠 Temporal (Orange)
*Happens After / Dependency*
Indicates a time-based sequence. The target record's timestamps are logically dependent on the source.
*Example: `Shipments` → `Deliveries`. A delivery cannot happen before a shipment is created.*

### 🟢 Lifecycle (Green)
*Creates or Updates*
Indicates that the target record represents a state change or a new phase in the source's lifecycle.
*Example: `Subscriptions` → `Billing`. A subscription lifecycle generates periodic billing events.*

### 🔴 Risk (Red)
*Problem or Anomaly*
Indicates a relationship used for monitoring, fraud detection, or error handling.
*Example: `Transactions` → `FraudAlerts`. Certain transaction patterns trigger risk assessments.*

### 🟡 Activity (Yellow)
*User Activity / Event Stream*
Indicates a high-volume stream of events generated by a source entity.
*Example: `Users` → `PageViews`. Users generate a continuous stream of activity data.*

## Why Semantics Matter?
These labels aren't just for show. They inform the **Simulation Engine** how to generate data. For instance, a "Temporal" relationship ensures that child dates are always after parent dates.
